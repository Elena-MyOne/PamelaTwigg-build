Back on the Playground

Something happened recently where I found myself back in school. Not in the classroom, but more on the playground metaphorically speaking, where old patterns of personality wars play out in the form of clicks and side conversations that plant seeds of doubt about the other. At first, I was taken aback by what was happening. Haven’t I moved on from those situations? Haven’t I grown and matured? And then something else happened – I had an ah-ha moment regarding one exchange in particular. I realized that my insecurities about fitting in, of being liked, had played out earlier in conversations that I was having about me feeling like a victim. Even though the awareness did not make me feel proud of my behavior, it did help me to understand what I should do to redirect the current conversations playing out. I picked up the phone and had an open and transparent conversation with the other person. It was hard to do but it turned out to be the right thing. It's hard to look in the mirror and understand what part you are playing in a conflict or a tense situation. It’s hard to see where you might be reverting to old, childish behavior. And at what cost to you? I like thinking that I’m growing, maturing, and shifting old destructive patterns into new rewarding ones. Live and learn.
