Back to Basics

When I work with a new client, I always start with the basics, regardless of what the client may want to achieve with coaching. Prior to our first session I send some questions. The questions are meant to trigger reflection on core values. By this, I don’t mean society’s values, or their family’s values, but their own. What a life well-lived means to the client. What their favorite activities are. What they are passionate about. Then we look at how satisfied they are in different areas of their lives. How does this compare to where their passion lies, who they are as a person, and how they want to spend their time? Often, there are surprises in areas they thought didn’t need attention. Having this awareness allows us to explore their intentions and the actions required for them to realize their intentions. The realization can be invigorating — confidence in decision-making grows, and a client feels the shift in other areas of life, creating more balance. Knowing who you are, what makes you tick and how you want to live are a good foundation to a successful coaching partnership.

Tools of the Trade

Recently I started watching some webinars particularly designed for those clients who have been diagnosed with ADHD. I find that the learning can apply to any of us. It might sound clichéd, but we all have too much going on, we all hate to say no, and we all want to be perfect in everything we do. I mean, who doesn’t, right? And when we’re less than perfect, there are plenty of reminders of how we fall short. The feeling of hustling for appreciation, for attention, for love is something we all recognize at some point in our lives. And it doesn’t have to be that way. One of the many tools that the founder and CEO of ihaveadhd.com, certified life coach Kristen Carder, talks about when feeling overwhelmed is SURF. SURF stands for Stop (just be quiet and let the emotion be used for unfolding), Unfold (create space for the emotion in your body), Recede(breathe) and Find a way to shift/Figure it out (why did you have the emotion, what are you learning from it). SURF teaches us how to be with emotions by:

1. Thinking Start observing your thoughts (until your brain is empty if need be)

2. Feeling Become grounded (or whatever emotion you love)

3. Doing what you want to do (this will only work when you think and feel first)

  1. Constrain Start cutting things off your list, ruthlessly
  2. Decide What do you want to do
  3. Schedule Schedule the activity by looking at your schedule, does it make sense based on your diary
  4. Execute Do it, make sure you are taking care of yourself and if you feel you can’t do it, go back to SURF on that emotion
