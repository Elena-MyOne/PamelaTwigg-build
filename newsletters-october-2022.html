Being Comfortable in the Space Between

Preparing for a half-marathon for me means that I have a lot of time to kill while I run. At first, I found listening to music to be the best distraction, but I’ve recently turned to listening to podcasts. Lots of them. There’s one that has stayed with me and has given me much food for thought and I wanted to share it with you. It’s a podcast by Brené Brown talking with psychotherapist Esther Perel, about paradox and straddling the tension of two competing ideas that can both be true. There’s a tool in coaching that is sometimes referred to as “Tensions of Presence.” When a client can identify and express where there are areas of tension, or paradox, being able to exist in the space between the two can be life changing. It also involves accepting that a situation doesn’t have to be either/or. It can be ‘and/both.’ As obvious as this may appear to some, it has been a concept that took me some time to wrap my head around. Being comfortable in the space that exists between two competing tensions doesn’t have to be confusing, scary, or complicated. It can be refreshing, expansive and comforting.

Tools of the Trade

One of the many requirements to becoming a certified coach involves receiving 6 hours of mentor coaching with at least 5 different mentor coaches. How it works: I record a session with one of my clients (after having received permission to do so) and submit an audiotape with analysis to a mentor, who then reviews the recording along with my notes and provides feedback. I hear about what went well and where I need to focus my efforts. In my most recent session, the mentor coach commented on my growth and my ability to pause. Pausing is a powerful tool. It’s one that I admittedly did not tend to employ when I started this journey 15 months ago. I have come to appreciate its strength. One of the best things a coach can do during a session with a client is pause. The space that is created allows the client to reflect, gain (or regain) perspective, possibly gain composure, and solidify a new awareness. It’s also a moment where creativity toward a solution to a challenge can happen.

The next time you find yourself in conversation with a friend, a colleague, a client, your child, or your partner/spouse, and you are noticing that you want to interrupt, rush ahead or jump to conclusions, try pausing. The power of pause can do wonders when you need to quiet that inner voice as well. My tool of the trade is your tip for the day. Just pause.
