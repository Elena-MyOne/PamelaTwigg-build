Outside the Box

See the Dreamer and the Renegade holding hands and smiling at one another as they skip toward the center of the stage. Their costumes are reminiscent of childhood days with “Raggedy Ann” like mismatched skirts and aprons flowing haphazardly over their legs as they tumble together in a fit of laughter, not caring or wondering how the other archetypes are observing them in their – what appears to be – careless play. The Dreamer and the Renegade intuitively pick up on the scene and wink knowingly to one another as they stand up, straighten their skirts, dust themselves off and look around at the circle of the remaining eight Archetypes who are looking back at them, each in anticipation of what is to come. The Dreamer has a way of looking compassionately into each one of the Archetypes’ eyes – almost as if she’s piercing their souls. This causes some to let out a slight gasp. The look seems to say, “I see what you are wanting. What you need. The Renegade and I are here to show you the way. Trust in us to intuitively choose the right moments to speak up, to disrupt and to imagine all that we can achieve when we pull together.” Notice the circle of Archetypes on the stage expand and relax as they begin to take in the collective energy of the Dreamer and the Renegade. It's as if they are only now starting to grasp the potential of what is possible for all of them if they take a step back and trust in the spontaneity of the moment that the Dreamer and the Renegade create.

The sound of the Outlook meeting notification comes crashing through like the sound of crystal breaking violently on a stone floor and jerks me somewhat hesitantly out of my own imagination of what can be when I invite the Dreamer and the Renegade to take my own center stage. The Strategist taps me on the shoulder and reminds me that what the Renegade and Dreamer have envisioned won’t happen unless I get to work. I smile as I say to myself that there’s always another intuitive idea waiting for me on my next run along the mountain trail as I turn to my computer, hunch back over my scribbled notes that I need to flesh out, notes that will eventually become that email I need to send about a workshop idea.
