Empathy? Yes, please.

“Erasing whole horizons of empathy” is a phrase I recently read, and it stuck with me. This was relating to the Second World War and how it was “not only a military conflict but an extraordinary assault on civilians” (Peter Fritzsche). It got me thinking about how necessary and powerful an emotion empathy is and how important it is to express it both personally and professionally. Coaches also have coaches, and I am working on accessing more of my Dreamer archetype with mine. There are many ways I can benefit from allowing my Dreamer to have more of a role in my life. One area is imaging a professional environment where empathy is a given, a strength in leadership. Imagine taking the time to show empathy (not sympathy) at work to a colleague who is stressed about a presentation and is waiting on data from someone else, or a disappointed colleague who just heard someone else got the promotion, or an emotionally drained colleague who is the main caregiver for a sick family member. I truly believe that empathy cascades to others. So, I’m thinking about my own work experiences. Am I curious to know what’s behind a colleague’s frustration or snippy response to a request? Do I take the time to find out? Do I stop and focus on who is in front of me at work? Do I really see them? Expanding horizons of empathy rather than erasing them? Yes, please.

Tools of the Trade

One of tools that I like to use at the beginning of a coaching contract is the “Wheel of Life Satisfaction.” A client will complete it at the beginning, halfway through, and at the end of our engagement together. It’s a great visual to see where a client may wish to focus based on how they rated an area of their life. I link the three points in time in a radar graph so the client can easily see shifts and development over time. Change occurs where there is intention. The intention drives choices and subsequent actions in the direction of a client’s desired outcome. Sometimes a client may have an area of development in mind before doing this quick assessment, and it’s not uncommon for the wheel of life to reflect a different reality from what they originally had in mind. If you’re interested in working with this tool, reply to this email or send me a text message and I’ll tell you more about it.
