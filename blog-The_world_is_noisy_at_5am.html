The world is noisy at 5am

I am not a morning person. I don’t mean that I’m the grumpy, ‘don’t speak to me until I’ve had coffee’ type, but given the opportunity, I will sleep in. (And by sleeping in, I mean 9am – 10am if I’m lucky.)

I’m also the type of person who would do well sleeping in a vault. No lights, no sound, no other movement. If any one of these conditions aren’t met, then I’m often tormented by my own awareness, hovering in that space where I’m neither fully asleep nor fully awake.

But there’s an upside. In these moments, I sometimes have what seem like major epiphanies. Like “I know the perfect 5-letter name for the ’name wordle’ my son mentioned earlier. “Travis!” Only, wait, Travis is six-letters. Something my husband pointed out after I proudly announced this revelation the next day. I decided that my brain removed a vowel and by doing so, I am incredibly clever. 

Spring had finally decided to enter the scene a few weeks ago and with the arrival of spring comes rebirth. And with rebirth, comes noise, particularly at 5am. A family of birds has built a nest in the space between my neighbor’s roof and gutter right outside my bedroom window, which means at exactly 5am, I am awakened by what I imagine to be a birds’ version of an operetta. They perform some sort of communicative ritual with other birds, resulting in a near Dolby-like surround sound concert, while I’m in the comfort (I use the term sarcastically) of my own bedroom. Adding to the bird operetta is the sound of a very long and rickety coal train screeching around the bend like the never-ending sound of nails or chalk on a whiteboard that make your front teeth ache when the sound is made.

This chaos repeated each morning this spring for what seemed like an eternity, with my annoyance growing. It was after about four mornings of this endless noise that I experienced another epiphany, this one maybe more profound. Yes, I can be cranky at the interruption of my precious sleep. On the other hand, I could revel in the fact that the world is noisy at 5am. Birds! Life! Activity! How lucky I am to be part of this continuum, the vibrant rhythm of life in what often seems like a world gone mad.
