Navigating uncertainty

I launch this first newsletter amid mixed emotions – hope, frustration, confusion, anger, sadness. And I am not alone. Like many others, I find myself looking for ways to navigate all the uncertainties and latch onto some solid reasons to hope for a better world, and lately I come up short. My husband sometimes accuses me of being self-centered, saying that “it’s all about Pamela.” In this case, it has to be! In a time of chaos, the only way for me to move forward without being overwhelmed by desperation and pessimism is to focus on myself and how to be more resilient. Tapping into our ability to be imaginative allows us to be expansive, which fuels positivity and creativity. Both are key to being resilient and allowing us to deal with uncertainty. I would love to hear what you’re doing to be resilient. Here are a few suggestions that come to mind:

Cloud watching – spend 5 minutes looking at what images and shapes roll across the sky

Skipping – see what happens to your mood when you sneak in a few skips during your next walk

Connecting with new and old friends – create a safe space for connection and calm. Due Quach, founder of Calm Clarity, refers to this as the ‘tend and befriend system’ as part of A Mind-Hacker's Toolkit to Calm Anxiety.

Listening to music – what role does music play in your life and how can it positively influence your mood? Spend a rainy (or balmy) afternoon listening to music, allowing yourself to roam through your earliest music memories.

Tools of the trade

Coaching is about helping someone, a team or an organization move forward to obtain the desired outcome. There are many tools and methods that can be used in a session to help with the process. They don’t have to be overly complicated and are – quite often – simple. Here’s one that I want to share with you, the Dialogic Orientation Quadrant by Haesun Moon, which is particularly useful for reflection and observation. I often use this myself, questioning where I might be positioned in the quadrant when I’m ‘stuck.’
